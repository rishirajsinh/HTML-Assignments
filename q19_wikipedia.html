Mutual fund statistics

The first introduction of a mutual fund in India occurred in 1963, when the Government of India launched the Unit Trust of India (UTI). Mutual funds are broadly categorised into three segments: equity funds, hybrid funds, and debt funds.[1]

  • The total Assets Under Management (AUM) of the Indian mutual fund industry as of December 31, 2023, stood at approximately ₹50.78 trillion (about US$600 billion).[2]
  • According to regulatory reporting, during FY 2022–23, many mutual fund units were redeemed within 2 years of investment; a small percentage continued for more than 5 years.[3]
  • Mutual funds attracted a share of household savings, while bank deposits and insurance remain dominant savings vehicles.[4]

Controversies

Over the years several incidents have affected investor confidence and resulted in regulatory action, scheme wind-ups and legal proceedings.

List of Mutual fund companies/schemes bankrupted, defaulted or closed

2020 Franklin Templeton Mutual Fund fiasco

In April 2020, Franklin Templeton India wound up six credit funds citing lack of liquidity amid the coronavirus pandemic. The action affected investors and led to regulatory probes by SEBI and subsequent penalties and restrictions on new debt schemes.[8]

Reliance Mutual Fund

In 2019, the debt schemes of Reliance Mutual Fund faced liquidity stress with forced redemptions and asset sales following exposure to troubled corporate debtors.[9]

IL&FS crisis and impact

The IL&FS default and downgrade events in 2018 had widespread impact on debt mutual funds and triggered regulatory changes to address concentration and liquidity risks.[10]

Amtek Auto Impact

Exposure to defaulting corporates like Amtek Auto caused some funds to mark down NAVs severely and take remedial steps including suspension of redemptions in extraordinary circumstances.[11]

2001 UTI Mutual Fund fiasco

The Unit Trust of India faced a major crisis in 2001 related to large redemptions, market manipulation scandals and a government-led restructuring that resulted in a bifurcation of UTI entities.[12]

Market segment

Mutual funds in India operate across market segments including equity, debt, hybrid, and money-market oriented funds. Fund houses manage schemes tailored to retail and institutional investors.

Average assets under management

Metrics such as AUM, flows, and redemption patterns are tracked periodically; regulators publish periodic reports used by analysts and investors to assess industry health.
